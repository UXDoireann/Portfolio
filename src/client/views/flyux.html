UX & UI Design

As part of a Professional Diploma in UX at the UX Design Institute, this project involved designing a mobile flight-booking app for a fictitious airline. From user research, to analysis, to design, prototyping and wireframing, I gained experience of the full life cycle of a UX project.



Step 1: Research

Competitive Benchmarking

To understand how best-in-class mobile apps solve the problem of creating a positive user experience of booking flights, I compared and reviewed four apps. I chose three well-regarded airline apps along with an accommodation booking app to see how a more indirect competitor designs for booking experiences. This proved to be an invaluable document that I referred back to on numerous occasions throughout the research, analysis and design phases.

Online Survey

I created an online survey to get a better understanding of people's goals when booking flights online and to find out:

  • If the last time they booked a flight, did they use a mobile app or desktop website?
  • Did they use an aggregator website as part of their search?
  • What website or mobile app did they use to make their booking and why?
  • Did this mobile app or website allow them to complete their booking easily?
  • If not, why not?

Usability Testing

I conducted three usability tests, each including an in-depth interview examining the user's habits and contexts surrounding their previous experiences of booking flights online. As FlyUX is a fictitious airline, I used two apps previously reviewed during Competitive Benchmarking: Turkish Airlines and Easyjet.

Listening to and observing these people search for and book flights was an incredibly interesting experience and provided a trove of insights and ideas. Throughout the overall design process, I kept these people in mind, thinking of their goals, their behaviours, their frustrations, their delight, their skepticism, their surprise and their stories.

Step 2: Analysis

Affinity Diagram

Having collected a great deal of qualitative research, the next step was to use an affinity diagram to analyse the data in-depth. Sharing my research with another person, collaboratively, we wrote our observations on post-its. Following this a process of organising and re-organising the post-its ensued. As there was a lot to analyze, we got really granular in our final groupings of data. While there is a lot to digest here, there were some key points that struck me....

  • A general distrust and skepticism of 'extras' and luggage costs and hence a need for a constant price clarity
  • As this project took place during Covid-19 pandemic, when searching for flights, lack of availability was often an issue. How an app handled this (or didn't!) was a key noteworthy point
  • Deviation from established conventions often caused confusion for users
  • Important information should be more forthcoming eg. layovers
